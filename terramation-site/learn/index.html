What is terramation?

Terramation, also known as human composting or natural organic reduction (NOR), is an eco-friendly end-of-life process that converts human remains into nutrient-rich soil. The process uses a vessel packed with organic materials — straw, wood chips, and wildflowers — to accelerate the natural process of decomposition. Within around 30 days, the body is fully transformed into approximately one cubic yard of usable soil.

The process is already legal in several US states including Washington, Colorado, California, and Oregon. In the UK, there is a growing campaign to legalise it — and in 2025 the UK government made its first official signal of support.
